Formula Racing Evolution

1946

Formula 1 regulations first established by the FIA, setting the foundation for modern single-seater racing.

1950

First official Formula 1 World Championship season begins, with Giuseppe Farina winning the inaugural title.

1967

Introduction of Formula 2 as a feeder series to develop young talent for Formula 1.

1971

Formula 3 gains international recognition as a crucial step in the racing ladder between karting and F2.

2001

Introduction of standardized chassis in F2 to reduce costs and emphasize driver skill over engineering budgets.

2014

First Formula E season begins, marking a new era of sustainable motorsport in city centers worldwide.

2015

Formula 4 launched as a global initiative to create affordable, standardized entry-level formula racing.

2023

Introduction of F1's new engine regulations focusing on sustainable fuels and increased electrification.
